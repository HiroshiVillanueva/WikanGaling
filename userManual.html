Introduction

WIKANGALING began as a school project created by the three of us — classmates who loved language and interactive design. We noticed younger students zoning out during language drills but lighting up for games. Our goal was simple: make language learning feel like play. This manual will walk you through every feature we built so teachers, parents, and students can get the most out of WIKANGALING.

Core Features

1. Sidebar

This is the Website's Sidebar! here you will be able to navigate through our pages and find everything you need to manage your Modules!

1. Page Panel

This is the Page panel, this is where you will be able to navigate through our 3 main webpage; The Home page, Module Editor, And the Dashboard where you will be able to view the attempt for all your modules.

2. Saves Panel

This is the Saves panel, here, you will be able to view all your modules and will be the primary area to open and edit your modules.

3. additional Buttons

Teachers can create a timed session where every student joins with a code. The teacher controls which mini-games run and can broadcast quick polls or pronunciation tasks live.

2. Module Editor

The Module Editor is where you will be able to create your very own modules! But before we get into creating a module, what even is a module? Well basically, it is the term we call for what people usually call- academic terms, a quiz or exam!
One Module = One Quiz Basically.

1. Module Creator

This is Module Editor! Here you will be able to modify you modules. This has the ability to add questions, delete them via sa x, Setting the answers for the questions, and adding in different types of questions.

3. Module Dashboard

Simple visual reports showing student attempts, mastery over the modules. You can see all your modules, and view all the people who have attempted them.

2. Show Attempts

This is the button that opens the information of all the students who took attempted the module.

3. Open Module

Opens the Quiz-Mode of the Module.

Getting Started — Creating your Account

Step 1 — Open Login Screen

There will always be a button saying "login" at the top-right corner on every webpage when load in.

Step 2 — Open The Registration View

At the bottom, there should be text saying "Don't have an account? Sign up". Press it and input your credentials. Make sure the email is valid!

Step 3 — Verify Email

Go ahead and Check the inbox of the email you used to register! There should a piece of mail there asking you to verify your account for WIKANGALING!

Getting Started — Making your first module:

Step 1 — Open The Module Editor

After logging into your account, head over to the module editor page

Step 2 — Creating A name for the Module

On the very top, you will see a name and a button that says "Create Module". Please press it after entering the name of your module.

Step 3 — Adding a Question

You will then see the Module Editor Open up, Here you will be able to add, edit, and delete your questions. Please Press the "add question" button to make your very first question.

Step 4 — Choosing a type

On the very top of each question container, you will see three buttons, Each of them represents one of the three questions types discussed earlier.

Step 5 — Adding an Option

Lastly, press the "Add option" button inside the question container and add in your very first option! It has 4 parts, the question itself, the set correct button, the remove, and the image importer.

> Remove button - is Instant, and will automatically remove your option from the question.

> Set Correct - Later on in the quiz mode, which ever you will set correct will be what the quiz will identify as the right answer and give points to the student.

> Import Image - This has two parts. The Image container and Image Upload button, the first is where you will import your image two. Then pressing the "Import Image" button will past that image into the option permanently.
